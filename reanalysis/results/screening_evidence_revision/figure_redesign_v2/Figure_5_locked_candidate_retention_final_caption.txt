Fig. 5. Locked external evaluation of a two-anchor candidate-retention baseline. (A) All candidates are assayed at two boundary conditions selected without response information; only the retained set continues across remaining conditions. (B) Source-level assay-unit reduction and observed-query-best retention. Values at right are retained/total query strata. (C) Panel-level query-best coverage versus assay-unit reduction; marker size reflects candidate count. (D) The locked failure in the Ogbuagu wheat-straw panel: deferred candidates had the highest recorded mean response at two intermediate Pb concentrations. (E) Descriptive retention-savings tradeoff; only the top-half rule was frozen before these external panels were screened. (F) Probability of retaining every simulated query best after independent Monte Carlo perturbation of cell means for panels with identifiable reported standard deviations. Panels and strata from the same source are not independent studies.
